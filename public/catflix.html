🐈 CATFLIX 🐈‍⬛

bMovies, rebranded for cats 😺. Same engine — eight AI agents, $0.99 pitch tier, 1B-supply royalty token, Stripe + BSV rails, on-chain artifacts — but every output is a cat movie 🎬🐱. Inspired by @catflixer 😻, who's already proving the thesis on X: there is a hungry, organised, monetisable audience for AI-generated cat content, and nobody has wrapped it in a tokenised production engine yet.

CATFLIX isn't a separate product. It's a vertical slice 🐾 of bMovies — proof that the engine is genre-agnostic and the wrapper is the only thing that needs to change to address a new market. The mogul/speculation framing on bmovies.online is one skin. The cat-content economy is another. Same plumbing 🐈.

The thesis 🐈

The engine is genre-agnostic. The wrapper isn't. 😼

bMovies the platform doesn't actually care that the films are Hollywood-style B-movies. The eight-agent pipeline — writer, director, storyboard, cinematographer, editor, composer, sound designer, producer — produces whatever the prompt asks for. Cat short. LEGO political satire. Memecoin music video. Religious parable. Brand-sponsored origin story. The pipeline doesn't know.

What changes per vertical is the narrative skin: the brand, the landing page, the example titles, the visual aesthetic of the studio cards, the personas of the eight founding agents, the genre defaults baked into the prompt. Everything below that — Stripe checkout, Supabase ledger, token mint, royalty cap table, x402 settlements, KYC gate at publish, Veriff integration — is reused as-is.

That makes every vertical a white-label opportunity. We can run them ourselves under our own brands (CATFLIX, HyperFlix, bOOvies), or we can sell rebranded deployments to operators who already own a niche audience but don't have an on-chain production engine.

Case study 01 🐱

CATFLIX — the cat-vertical 😻

Cat content is one of the largest, most consistently monetisable verticals on the open internet 🐈. @catflixer on X is proving the AI version of it — short-form, character-driven, replayable, sharable cat shorts that travel because they're cats 😹. The unmet need: a way to monetise that beyond views.

CATFLIX wraps the bMovies engine for that audience. A creator pays $0.99 to pitch ("Mr Whiskers becomes Mayor" 🐱👔), and an eight-agent crew of cat-themed AI personas (the writer is "Hemingway Tabby" 😺, the director is "Wong Cat-Wai" 😼, the composer is "Hans Zimmer-Cat" 😽) produces a poster + treatment + token in 60 seconds. Tier up to a trailer ($9.99), short ($99), or feature ($999). Each film is a tradeable BSV-21 token; the audience funds the next tier by buying royalty shares. The leaderboard tracks which cat universe is winning 🏆.

Why this works 😸: the cat-content audience already understands "viral video as collectible" — they buy NFT cats 🐈‍⬛, follow cat-only Twitter accounts 🐾, support cat creators on Patreon. The piece they're missing is a primary-issuance market for cat films. CATFLIX is that market, with the bMovies cap-table mechanics doing the heavy lifting underneath 😻.
Case study 02

The Iran LEGO moment

While CATFLIX is the friendly version of this thesis, the political-memetic version is just as live. Iran's recent series of LEGO stop-motion videos trolling Trump hit massive global reach — millions of views, picked up by mainstream media, shared across the political spectrum. The format is replicable, the production cost was tiny, and the strategic value (cultural soft power, narrative-setting, agenda framing) was enormous.

That's a market. Not a market we'd run ourselves — we don't want to be a state-actor's propaganda arm — but a category we'd be foolish not to recognise. Political-memetic film, especially in stop-motion / LEGO / animated form, is a vertical with proven distribution and proven demand. Anyone running a political movement, a satirical media outlet, an activist campaign, or a hostile-state communication function has a use for an on-chain film engine. The bMovies pipeline could power any of them.

The honest framing for our BD list: this category exists, it's huge, and the buyers are not Hollywood. Pricing it accordingly — and choosing which of those buyers we want to do business with — is a separate conversation.

Case study 03 🐾

HyperFlix — already partly built

We've already started one of these verticals: HyperFlix (live at hyperflix.com · source at /Volumes/2026/Projects/hyperflix) is the memecoin-video market. Same insight as CATFLIX, applied to memecoin culture. A Next.js app with HandCash + Solana wallet adapters, partial UI, no production engine wired up yet.

The HyperFlix backlog is exactly this: port the bMovies architecture — agent pipeline, Stripe webhook, token mint, cap table, royalty splits — and reskin the front end. Re-using the engine means we can have HyperFlix shipping memecoin-video commissions in days rather than months, instead of building the whole pipeline from scratch.

HyperFlix is the proof that we're not just theorising about white-label. We started building it for memecoins before we built bMovies. The discipline now is: finish bMovies, then port the architecture sideways, not start three half-finished platforms. → Open the HyperFlix page.

What gets reused

One engine, many front ends

Every vertical above shares the same backend stack. The differentiator is the landing page, the studio brand, the agent personas, and the prompt-tuning. A white-label deployment is roughly a fork of /public + a tenant config row in Supabase. Pipeline, payments, KYC, treasury, on-chain mints — all shared.

01

Pitch

$0.99 Stripe checkout, /api/refine for AI logline polish, /api/checkout for the buy. Same flow regardless of vertical.

02

Production

Eight AI agents on the production floor. Personas per vertical (cat-themed, LEGO-themed, faith-themed) are config, not new code.

03

Token mint

BSV-21 1B-supply royalty token per film, 99% to commissioner. Identical contract per vertical.

04

Cap table

Bonding-curve fund page, KYC gate at list-shares, x402 settlements per agent step. Backend identical.

BD framing: we're not selling films. We're selling a film-production-and-cap-table-as-a-service stack with a brand wrapper. Each white-label customer pays a setup fee + a per-film platform cut. Their audience pays the commission tiers. We keep 1% of every film made on every deployment, forever.
