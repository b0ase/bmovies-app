Chapter 01 · Let's get the vibe straight

Is this a meme coin?

We've been asked. The honest answer is: no, but it rhymes. The things you trade on bMovies are royalty shares in actual films, not fractions of a dog. But the launch mechanic — cheap, permissionless, audience-curated — is absolutely the Pump.fun move. Here's the split.

not a meme coin

Every royalty share maps to a real film with a real production pipeline, a real treatment, a real poster, and a real revenue stream from real viewers paying $2.99 for tickets. The "coin" has a movie attached.

When a holder earns, it's because somebody pressed play. Not because the graph went up. Payouts are per stream, per ticket, forever. If the film flops, the share earns nothing — but the share isn't invented, it's a fractional claim on something that exists.

absolutely vibes like one

Launch price is dirt cheap. Anybody can pitch. The market decides which films graduate by commissioning the next tier. The bonding curve is a ladder of real productions, not a liquidity pool — but the energy is the same: rapid iteration, loud memes, a graduation ceremony when you win.

We're pointing a firehose of Pump.fun-style degen attention at the one industry that's always needed it: film finance. The people who speedran coin culture just got handed a poster generator.

Chapter 04 · The graduation ceremony

Every film that climbs the full ladder to feature drops on Sunday at 21:00 UTC. The marketplace gets a graduation banner, the holders get a notification, and the premiere goes live with the full credit roll. If you bought in at the pitch rung for $0.99, your name is in that roll.

Chapter 06 · What shares actually pay

Real receipts, not candle charts.

The share's value isn't its tick — it's the stream of ticket royalties underneath. Every ticket ($2.99) flows straight to holders pro-rata. Every view on a pay-per-stream page does too. bMovies takes 1%. That's it. No platform fee on trades. No "protocol revenue split". Just a 1% slice on day one that stays 1% on day one thousand.

The chart can pump or dump all it wants — underneath is a film with a revenue line. You can screenshot the candles. You can also just press play.

⚠ Silly brochure, serious risk disclosure

Royalty shares are speculative. If a film doesn't sell tickets, the share earns nothing. If a film never graduates past the pitch tier, the treatment still exists but nobody's watching. Early holders can be left holding supply when a project stalls — that's exactly what happens in Pump.fun too, we've just swapped a bonding curve for a tier ladder.

This isn't financial advice. It's a Ponzinomics pastiche with the volume turned up. The underlying platform is real, the BSV rail is real, the 99%/1% split is real, and the films are real. The rocket emojis are artistic licence.
