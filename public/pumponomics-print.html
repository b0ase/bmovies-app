Chapter 01 · Let's get the vibe straight
Is it a meme coin?

Short answer: no, but it rhymes. The things you buy on bMovies are royalty shares in actual films, not fractions of a dog. But the launch mechanic — cheap, permissionless, audience-curated — is absolutely the Pump.fun move. Here's the split.

not a meme coin

Every royalty share maps to a real film with a real production pipeline, a real treatment, a real poster, and a real revenue stream from real viewers paying $2.99 per ticket. The "coin" has a movie attached.

When a holder earns, it's because somebody pressed play. Payouts are per stream, per ticket, forever. If the film flops, the share earns nothing — but the share isn't invented, it's a fractional claim on something that exists.

vibes like one

Launch price is dirt cheap. Anybody can pitch. The market decides which films graduate by commissioning the next tier. The bonding curve is a ladder of real productions, not a liquidity pool — but the energy is the same: rapid iteration, loud memes, a graduation ceremony when you win.

We pointed a firehose of Pump.fun-style degen attention at the one industry that's needed it for a hundred years: film finance.

The hook in one line:

"It's Pump.fun for movies." Three words. Your audience already knows: anyone can launch, the market picks the winners, graduation is the promise. You don't have to explain agents, royalty shares, or BSV to sell the front door. The back door is actually a film studio.

Chapter 04 · Pick your archetype
Which degen
are you?

Four clean archetypes. None of them mutually exclusive. Pick one to start and rotate when you get bored. Every archetype makes money a slightly different way — and loses money a slightly different way too. Pick the one that matches your attention span.

🎤
THE LAUNCHER

Idea guy

You pitch. A lot. Most don't pump. The one that does pays for the other 99. You're the Pump.fun coin-maker, but the "coin" is a screenplay.

🚀
THE PUMPER

Tier-climber

You hunt pitches at the bottom of the ladder and commission the trailer before the crowd gets there. Upside baked in, if you've got taste.

💎
THE HOLDER

Diamond hands

You like the film. You hold the shares. You don't sell on the pump — you sit through the premiere and collect ticket royalties forever. Classic.

🎬
THE GRADUATE

Mogul-in-training

You ladder one project from $0.99 to $999, funding each rung with the last. By Monday morning, you're a studio owner with a cinema release.

The uncomfortable truth

A Launcher who hits one winner a quarter beats a Pumper who's 80% right but loses on the 20%. A Holder who sits through a premiere beats both of them over 18 months. The Graduate is the Cinderella story the marketing writes about — but Holders are the ones who actually build a portfolio. Stack roles. Don't just pick one.

⚠ The honesty section (required reading, only two paragraphs long)

Royalty shares are speculative. If a film doesn't sell tickets, the share earns nothing. If a film never graduates past the pitch tier, the treatment still exists but nobody's watching. Early holders can be left holding supply when a project stalls — that's exactly what happens in Pump.fun too. We've just swapped a bonding curve for a tier ladder and a graduation ceremony for a rugpull.

This isn't financial advice. It's a Ponzinomics pastiche with the volume turned up. The underlying platform is real. The BSV rail is real. The 99%/1% split is real. The films are real.
