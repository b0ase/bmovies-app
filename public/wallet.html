Getting started

Your wallet is your identity.

bMovies is a BRC-100 app. That means every payment — watching a film, commissioning a production, investing in a project — is signed and broadcast by your wallet, not our servers. We never touch your private keys. Your wallet address IS your identity, and every transaction you make is verifiable on-chain forever.

To use bMovies you need a BRC-100 compatible wallet running on your machine. The recommended option is BSV Desktop — a free, open-source desktop wallet built by the BSV Blockchain Association.

What is BRC-100?

The open wallet standard for BSV apps.

BRC-100 is a vendor-neutral interface that lets any app talk to any wallet — like MetaMask for Ethereum, but for Bitcoin SV. Instead of each app building its own wallet integration, BRC-100 defines a single open API. Your keys stay in your wallet. Apps request payments via createAction. You approve or reject in the wallet UI.

Supported wallets: BSV Desktop (recommended), Metanet Client, Yours Wallet (browser extension). All speak the same BRC-100 protocol.

Once connected, your wallet works across all BRC-100 apps — not just bMovies. Films you fund, tokens you hold, royalties you earn — all tied to your wallet address and visible on-chain.

Step 2 — Try a payment

Send a micro-payment

This sends a real BSV payment from your wallet to confirm everything works. The default is 100 sats (~$0.016) — less than a penny. Your wallet will open a confirmation dialog; approve it there. The transaction is broadcast to on-chain and you'll get a WhatsOnChain link to verify it.
