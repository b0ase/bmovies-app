Marketing ideas

Different hooks for the same thing. bMovies is the cheapest way to make a movie the world has ever seen — but "cheapest way to make a movie" isn't a campaign. These are. Each one pulls a different lever: the cheeky cut, the degen cut, the mogul cut. Pick one, steal from another, mash them together.

Campaign 01 · The cheeky cut

bOOvies

The adults-only spin-off. Same engine, same agents, same 99% ownership — but the poster has tits on it. A pink-neon parody of the main brand that lets us market to a massive, underserved audience without polluting the family logo.

Six founding parody titles already teed up: Boobfather, Boob-ning, Jurassic Jugs, Raiders of the Lost Rack, Pulp Friction, Boob Wars. Each one its own standalone landing page.

Campaign 02 · The degen cut

Pump.fun for movies

Position bMovies as the film-industry answer to Pump.fun. A $0.99 pitch is a launch. The audience "pumps" the winners up the tier ladder — trailer, short, feature — buying royalty shares along the way. Graduation replaces greenlighting.

Ships with a silly-serious brochure — PUMPONOMICS — modelled on our Ponzinomics guide but cranked up: rocket emojis, graduation ceremonies, and the pump-and-screen cycle, in A4 PDF glory.

How we think about these

These aren't separate businesses. They're narrative skins over the same engine: a studio marketplace where any user can pitch a film for $0.99, ride a 28-step agent pipeline, and keep 99% of the royalty shares. The page the audience lands on is the only thing that changes. Same backend, same BSV rail, same artifacts — different story told on the way in.
